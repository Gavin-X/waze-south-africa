Welcome & Getting Started

Your initial entry into South Africa's live map community[cite: 501, 505].

What is Waze?

Waze functions as a completely free community GPS tool[cite: 507]. Beyond standard mapping, it reads localized road names out loud via text-to-speech, checks for speed traps or accidents, recalculates transit routes to drop commute windows, and allows drivers to avoid toll infrastructure safely[cite: 507, 508, 509, 510, 511]. It operates efficiently via direct crowd-sourced data[cite: 512, 513]. Every active commute feeds structural map metrics, and manual edits translate directly into smoother travel times across South Africa[cite: 514, 515].

The Golden Rule: Usability, Simplicity, Retention

Ensure all modifications explicitly aid routing logic, keep visual geometry clean, and never delete road segments blindly — deletion completely wipes out historic traffic speed metrics built over multiple years[cite: 518, 519, 520, 521].

Understanding Your Boundaries

You are constrained to modifying your specific "Editable Area"[cite: 540]. This boundary encompasses the physical tracks you have traveled with the live app switched on within the past 90 days[cite: 541, 544]. As your editor rank increases, the allowed editing buffer radius surrounding those driven paths scales up[cite: 542]. Hit Shift+E within the map console to easily toggle your active region visibility layer[cite: 545].

Road Classifications & Naming

Standardizing names for seamless Text-To-Speech (TTS) integration[cite: 627, 961, 962].

The Functional Hierarchy

Freeway (120 km/h baseline) [cite: 693] Fully access-controlled dual carriageways linked exclusively via ramps, with zero stop signs or property driveway interfaces[cite: 633]. Restricted to official N, R, or M identifiers[cite: 634]. Must be constructed as independent, parallel single-direction paths[cite: 635]. Format example: N1 N or De Villiers Graaff Motorway (M1 N)[cite: 636, 637].
Major Highway (100–120 km/h) [cite: 693] Heavy-capacity arterials connecting cities or districts that are not fully access-controlled[cite: 641, 642]. Incorporates two-digit national or provincial paths[cite: 641, 643]. Prefix addition is mandatory: Main Reef Rd (R41 W)[cite: 645].
Minor Highway (80–100 km/h) [cite: 693] Three-digit regional intersections (Rxxx) primarily managed via intersections and traffic lights[cite: 647, 648]. Example formatting: Kliprivier Dr (R556 S)[cite: 649].
Primary Street & Suburban Streets Primary Streets represent the core distribution arteries traversing suburban centers (e.g., Jan Smuts Ave)[cite: 651, 652]. Standard residential pathways take the basic Street tag[cite: 654].

TTS Optimization Suffix Abbreviations

To maintain voice processing consistency, utilize the English suffix notation with formal capital casing[cite: 668, 670]. For abbreviations of titles embedded in the title block, include a trailing dot separator (St. for Saint, Dr. for Doctor)[cite: 672].

Road → Rd [cite: 675]
Street → St [cite: 675]
Avenue → Ave [cite: 675]
Drive → Dr [cite: 675]
Crescent → Cres [cite: 675]
Close → Spell Out [cite: 675]

The Address City Rule

Waze processes a unified regional address block[cite: 677]. In South Africa, enforce the clean Suburb, City string layout[cite: 677]:

Creating & Modifying Layouts

Manual segment construction and network connectivity guidelines[cite: 705, 706].

Constructing a New Path

  1. Initialize mapping by pressing I[cite: 718]. The mouse pointer shifts to a blue marker[cite: 719].
  2. Plot points along the center path of the satellite footprint to create structural nodes[cite: 722, 723]. Map curved features progressively; use only two points for pure straights[cite: 724].
  3. Double-click at your end coordinate to wrap configuration[cite: 726]. Unsaved items render in a sharp, unconfirmed red line[cite: 726, 946].
  4. Ensure structural points bridge perfectly[cite: 729]. Circular markers confirm solid junction creation; square markers flag an open, disconnected break[cite: 730].
⚠️ Data Discard Hazard: You must hit the Apply configuration block manually inside the property editor window after updating address variables or road labels[cite: 735, 739]. Navigating away or clicking the map background immediately deletes your text changes without throwing a warning prompt[cite: 739, 794].

Level Layering (Elevation Management)

To bypass structural map conflict loops, physical paths that overlap without physically intersecting requires discrete layer tagging[cite: 749, 980]:

Level -1 / -2 (Tunnels / Underpasses) [cite: 748, 980]

Managing Toll Portals Correctly

South Africa manages large, localized commercial toll operations (including SANRAL, Bakwena, N3TC, TRAC, and Entilini networks)[cite: 825, 826]. To keep routing calculations accurate:

  • Toggle the Toll Road parameter checkbox ONLY on the short individual segment intersecting the physical collections gate or gantry platform[cite: 822].
  • Never check the toll property down the entire runtime length of a multi-kilometer highway — doing so breaks alternative, non-toll routing options[cite: 823].
  • Slice an explicit node point precisely at the payment station block to offset false automated traffic congestion reports triggered by queuing drivers[cite: 824].

Core Best Practices

Operational standards compiled by veteran local community contributors[cite: 843, 844].

Preserve Speed History Data When introducing a fresh intersecting link into an already established suburban arterial road, always choose to split the current segment directly rather than laying down a new line from scratch[cite: 870]. Splitting safely locks in historic point data speed metrics; completely rebuilding a sector wipes past history, throwing off route timing estimates[cite: 794, 871].
Handling Renamed South African Streets Suburban tracks across the country are regularly updated to honor historic local figures[cite: 901]. When names change, update the Primary Label block to correspond to physical street signs, and relegate the past label name to the Alternative Name slot until the municipality replaces all roadside signage[cite: 901, 902].

Why It Matters: Real-World Impact

Understanding that editing is more than just a video game[cite: 932].

Waze has a direct, tangible effect on the daily lives and security of real South Africans[cite: 933]. Clean data formatting translates into critical real-world improvements:

Emergency Services

Accurate layout mapping helps emergency vehicles navigate informal settlements or pass security check-points in complex gated developments when seconds count[cite: 934].

Commuter Optimization

Balancing neighborhood street rules against primary access avenues helps reduce heavy gridlock around school districts during morning peak travel windows[cite: 935].

Financial Protection

Mapping traditional toll plazas, remaining gantries, and rough unpaved roads correctly helps drivers avoid unexpected fees and excessive vehicle wear-and-tear[cite: 936].

Case Study: The Gated Estate Trap

What happens when Waze attempts to route an open delivery driver down a restricted, resident-only service entrance at complexes like Dainfern or Woodmead[cite: 937]? This occurs when an editor accidentally leaves an entry gate open on the map[cite: 937]. This misconfiguration causes major issues for drivers, which can be avoided by using the Private Road classification[cite: 662, 937].

Conquering the Fear Barrier

Adopting the guardian mindset to prevent map errors[cite: 938, 941].

The most common reason new contributors step away is the fear of breaking live data[cite: 939]. Experienced local editors emphasize that mistakes happen to everyone, and minor issues can be easily corrected[cite: 940, 943].

The Golden Rule of Connectivity

Never blindly connect non-drivable segments (such as walking trails, pedestrian boardwalks, or active railroad lines) directly into the main drivable road network[cite: 944].

Critical Error Impact: If you link these layers incorrectly, the routing engine will try to navigate standard commuter cars directly down active railway lines[cite: 945].

The Investigation Framework

Five steps of routing detective work before submitting edits[cite: 947, 949].

Don't simply edit road elements right away[cite: 948]. Run a complete diagnostic check across the problem area to understand exactly why the routing engine is generating specific instructions[cite: 948].

01
Analyze GPS Coordinate Tracks

Toggle on the raw GPS layer in WME (Shift+G) to view where drivers are physically traveling[cite: 602, 853, 950].

02
Cross-Reference via Livemap Portal

Run point-to-point pathing tests within the live engine to see how it calculates routes through the problem spot[cite: 855, 951].

03
Verify Street View & Physical Reality

Check real-world details like official municipal signage, painted directional arrows, and physical lane limitations[cite: 852, 952].

04
Consult the Community via Slack

Share a permalink in the #south-africa channel if the existing layout logic looks confusing[cite: 527, 606, 953].

05
Publish Your Changes

Apply your modifications only once your map evidence aligns cleanly with local traffic rules[cite: 954].

The Great Dirt Road Penalty Framework

Unpaved farm routes or rural dirt links should be classified under the standard Street category, but you must manually check the Unpaved attribute box[cite: 975, 976]. This automatically triggers a specialized routing penalty that keeps standard low-clearance vehicles from driving down rough paths unless the user toggles on "Allow Dirt Roads" in their mobile app settings[cite: 977].

Intellectual Property Boundary Rule: Never trace new streets or copy layout details directly from Google Maps or Google aerial imagery[cite: 985]. This violates copyright regulations and introduces alignment issues in gated zones[cite: 986]. Instead, rely on Waze's licensed imagery feeds or official open-government options like the Surveyor General database (csg.dla.gov.za)[cite: 841, 842, 987].
