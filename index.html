Welcome & Getting Started

Your initial entry into South Africa's live map community.

What is Waze?

Waze functions as a completely free community GPS tool. Beyond standard mapping, it reads localized road names out loud via text-to-speech, checks for speed traps or accidents, recalculates transit routes to drop commute windows, and allows drivers to avoid toll infrastructure safely. It operates efficiently via direct crowd-sourced data. Every active commute feeds structural map metrics, and manual edits translate directly into smoother travel times across South Africa.

The Golden Rule: Usability, Simplicity, Retention

Ensure all modifications explicitly aid routing logic, keep visual geometry clean, and never delete road segments blindly — deletion completely wipes out historic traffic speed metrics built over multiple years.

Community Onboarding

The regional team lives and collaborates within Slack at wazeinternational.slack.com. Use the direct link bit.ly/WazeIntSlack to hop inside and join the main #south-africa communication channel. This platform is used for real-time unlock operations, troubleshooting, and coordinating with localized Area and Country Managers.

Understanding Your Boundaries

You are constrained to modifying your specific "Editable Area". This boundary encompasses the physical tracks you have traveled with the live app switched on within the past 90 days. As your editor rank increases, the allowed editing buffer radius surrounding those driven paths scales up. Hit Shift+E within the map console to easily toggle your active region visibility layer.

Sharing Permalinks Correctly

When signaling an issue on Slack or lodging an unlock call on the regional board, never paste a generic web browser address. Center your map over the problem zone, highlight the specific node or street segment, click the Permalink chain icon positioned along the top header bar, and copy that direct map reference link.

Road Classifications & Naming

Standardizing names for seamless Text-To-Speech (TTS) integration.

The Functional Hierarchy

Freeway (120 km/h baseline) Fully access-controlled dual carriageways linked exclusively via ramps, with zero stop signs or property driveway interfaces. Restricted to official N, R, or M identifiers. Must be constructed as independent, parallel single-direction paths. Format example: N1 N or De Villiers Graaff Motorway (M1 N).
Major Highway (100–120 km/h) Heavy-capacity arterials connecting cities or districts that are not fully access-controlled. Incorporates two-digit national or provincial paths. Prefix addition is mandatory: Main Reef Rd (R41 W).
Minor Highway (80–100 km/h) Three-digit regional intersections (Rxxx) primarily managed via intersections and traffic lights. Example formatting: Kliprivier Dr (R556 S).
Primary Street & Suburban Streets Primary Streets represent the core distribution arteries traversing suburban centers (e.g., Jan Smuts Ave). Standard residential pathways take the basic Street tag.

TTS Optimization Suffix Abbreviations

To maintain voice processing consistency, utilize the English suffix notation with formal capital casing. For abbreviations of titles embedded in the title block, include a trailing dot separator (St. for Saint, Dr. for Doctor).

Road → Rd
Street → St
Avenue → Ave
Drive → Dr
Crescent → Cres
Close → Spell Out
