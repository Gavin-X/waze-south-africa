Why It Matters: Real-World Impact

Understanding that editing maps has direct everyday consequences.

Waze relies heavily on crowdsourced intelligence, which means your edits directly touch the lives and personal safety of real drivers across the nation. This isn't just data management—it's community responsibility.

🚑 Emergency Services

Proper layout design helps emergency responders smoothly navigate dense informal settlements or clear security gates in complex residential areas quickly and safely.

🚗 Commuter Balancing

Differentiating local suburban pathways from major throughput avenues helps ease gridlock around school boundaries during chaotic rush hour periods.

💰 Financial Protection

Mapping commercial toll plazas and unpaved, rough dirt sectors properly lets drivers choose routes that shield their wallets and vehicle maintenance budgets.

📕 Case Study: The Gated Estate Trap

Leaving an estate gate set wide open on the map means the navigation engine will try to send general delivery drivers down strict, gated-access-only driveways. This creates confusion and safety concerns. Proper mapping prevents this cascade.

Conquering the Fear Barrier

Shifting to a protective guardian mindset to manage live map updates safely.

Many fresh contributors stop editing because they are worried about making mistakes on a live map. Veteran editors emphasize that slip-ups happen to everyone—and the community has safeguards to catch them.

⚡ The Golden Rule of Connectivity

Never snap non-drivable elements like footpaths, walking paths, or active railway lines directly to drivable road segments.

The Direct Impact: Incorrectly connecting these lines can trick the system into routing everyday commuter traffic right onto a railway line.

The Investigation Framework

Five steps of map discovery work before applying an update.

Avoid jumping straight into edits without a plan. Spend time reviewing the surrounding context to understand why the routing system behaves the way it does.

01
📍 Analyze GPS Coordinate Tracks

Toggle on the raw GPS layer in WME (Shift+G) to see exactly where drivers are physically traveling.

02
🗺️ Cross-Reference via Livemap

Run point-to-point pathing tests in the live user engine to see how the software routes traffic through the area.

03
📷 Verify Street View & Real-World Context

Look for key physical realities, such as street name signs, painted lane boundaries, and official turn restrictions.

04
💬 Consult the Community via Slack

Drop a permalink into the local Slack space if you run into confusing layout structures.

05
✅ Publish Your Edits Safely

Apply your layout changes only after your map evidence aligns cleanly with local laws and signs.

⚠️ Case Study: The Ghost Turn Trap If the application recommends an illegal or unsafe right turn over multiple lanes of fast traffic, look at the intersection node closely. You can resolve this issue by uncovering a hidden one-way restriction or mis-aligned node connection.

Your initial entry into South Africa's live map community.

🧭 What is Waze?

Waze functions as a community-driven navigation ecosystem. Beyond standard pathing, it reads local street titles aloud using clear text-to-speech voice synthesis, helping drivers stay focused on the road ahead.

✨ The Golden Rule: Usability, Simplicity, Retention

Ensure all updates improve routing, keep visual geometry clean, and never delete road segments blindly — deletion completely wipes them from live servers.

Road Classifications & Naming

Standardizing names for seamless Text-To-Speech (TTS) voice alerts.

🏗️ The Functional Hierarchy

🛣️ Freeway (120 km/h baseline) Fully access-controlled dual carriageways joined only by ramps, with zero stop signs or direct private property driveways. Restricted to official N, R, or M identifiers, mapped with appropriate grade separation.
🛣️ Major Highway (100–120 km/h) High-capacity regional roads connecting towns that are not fully access-controlled. Requires double-digit route labels: M10, M1, etc.
🛣️ Minor Highway (80–100 km/h) Three-digit regional avenues handled mainly via intersections and traffic lights. Example structure: Kliprivier Dr (R556 alternate).
🏘️ Primary Street & Suburban Streets Primary Streets cover core suburban connector networks (e.g., Jan Smuts Ave). Quiet residential streets receive the Street classification by default.

🔤 TTS Suffix Abbreviations

To keep voice engine output uniform, use English suffix indicators with proper capital styling. For formal title prefixes inside name lines, include proper spacing.

Road → Rd
Street → St
Avenue → Ave
Drive → Dr
Crescent → Cres
Close → Spell Out

📮 The Address City Rule

Addresses use a clear, unified format. For South African sectors, enforce the clean Suburb, City layout consistently across all regions.

  • ✅ Correct: Morningside, Sandton
  • ❌ Incorrect: Morningside, Johannesburg (Outdated municipal structure labels)
  • ❌ Incorrect: Morningside, Sandton, Gauteng (Avoid stacking redundant province values)

Creating & Modifying Layouts

Manual segment construction and path connectivity guidelines.

✏️ Drawing a New Segment

  1. Start map drawing by pressing I. Your mouse cursor turns into a blue point.
  2. Drop points down the true center of the satellite footprint to create geometry nodes. Build curves progressively; keep pure straights down to two simple points.
  3. Double-click your final point to finish drawing. Unsaved segments appear as a bright red line.
  4. Check that your connections connect cleanly. Circular node markers show established joints; square markers show disconnected breaks.
⚠️ Data Loss Hazard: You must click the Apply configuration option within the sidebar panel manually after making any adjustments to names or classifications—changes do not auto-save.

📐 Level Layers (Elevation Control)

To prevent routing logic errors, segments that cross without a physical connection require independent layer values:

Level 0 (Standard Ground Level)
Level +1 / +2 (Bridges & Flyovers)
Level -1 / -2 (Tunnels & Subways)
Level -5 (Non-Drivable Corridors)

🔄 The Q+W Node Reset Step

To completely clear out invisible, automated soft restrictions that can cause navigation errors down the line, apply this sequence to every fresh segment node:

// Select the core raw node point, then perform sequence:

1. Press Q → Hard restricts all connecting choices (all arrows flash red).

2. Press W → Resets and opens every path simultaneously (all vectors turn green).

// 3. Manually click and close any forbidden movements prohibited by local traffic signs.

Security Locks, Places & Toll Networks

Protecting live segment data and tracking toll checkpoints.

🔒 South African Segment Lock Blueprint

Locking protects verified layouts from accidental damage caused by new accounts. Apply locks only after confirming that the geometry, naming, and connectivity are all correct.

Segment Type Classification Required Minimum Lock Assignment
Freeways (National N-Paths) & connecting ramp systemsLevel 5
Mainline Physical Toll Gate / Toll Plaza areasLevel 5
Major Highways (Double-digit N or R routes)Level 4
Minor Highways (Triple-digit R routes)Level 3
Primary Streets (Core suburban distribution lines)Level 2
Standard Streets (Residential neighborhood avenues)Level 1 (Default Entry)
Note: Level 6 system locks are not dynamically utilized in South Africa due to a lack of active administrative deployment at the country level.

💳 Managing Toll Portals Correctly

South Africa manages large regional toll systems. To maintain accurate pricing and routing estimates:

  • Check the Toll Road attribute box ONLY on the short, distinct line that crosses the physical collection plaza or collection gantry.
  • Never check the toll box along the entire length of a long highway — doing so breaks alternative, non-toll routing math.
  • Slice an explicit node point right at the payment barrier to balance out false congestion warnings caused by queuing drivers.

Core Best Practices

Operational guidelines compiled by veteran community managers.

⚡ Preserve Speed History Data When inserting a new cross street connection into an existing major arterial road, always split the active segment directly instead of drawing a line out of nowhere. Splitting preserves the historical speed profiles and driver behavior data.
🔄 Handling Renamed South African Streets Suburban roads across the country are often updated to honor historic figures. When names change, update the Primary Label line to match new physical signs, and move the older street name into the Alternate Name field for historical reference.
