Blueprint | Module 4

How to Fix Things (The Bread & Butter Edits)

Now that you know how to investigate, here is how you safely repair common issues without changing road hierarchies.

Turn Restrictions & Directionality

The Arrows: Green means go; Red means restricted. Setting these accurately to match local municipal traffic signs prevents illegal/dangerous routing.

One-Way vs. Two-Way: Ensuring suburban backstreets allow dual-direction traffic while dual-carriageways/freeways are mapped as parallel one-way segments.

SA Naming Standards (TTS Optimization)

Waze speaks the road names out loud using Text-To-Speech (e.g., "Turn left into Elizabeth Ave").

The Suffix Rule: Always use the standard capitalized English abbreviations.
Correct: Elizabeth Ave, Main Rd, Smith St.
Incorrect: Elizabeth Avenue, Main rd, Smith st.

The Localization Rule: Always include the Suburb/City name so traffic alerts say "Accident on Rivonia Rd, Sandton" rather than just an ambiguous "Accident on Rivonia Rd".

The South African Road Hierarchy

Waze uses standard dictionary/functional definitions for roads, not strictly legal ones.

Localized Mapping Challenges

The Great Dirt Road Penalty: How Waze handles unpaved rural or farm roads. Mark them as Street but check the Unpaved box. This applies an automatic routing penalty for normal cars unless the driver explicitly toggles "Allow Dirt Roads" in-app.

Parking Lot Restraint: Shopping center and parkade layouts. Do not map every single lane. Map only the primary entry/exit tracks and main thoroughfares to avoid getting your edits flagged or cleaned up by Area Managers.

Bridges & Level Layering: Ground level is Level 0. Overpasses and underpasses must use alternating levels (1, 2, -1) so Waze knows the roads cross without physically intersecting.

Welcome & Getting Started

Your first steps into the SA Waze editing community

What is Waze?

Waze is a free GPS navigation app for your smartphone. Beyond just giving you directions, it:

  • Provides spoken turn-by-turn directions with street names (Text-to-Speech)
  • Shows real-time traffic conditions and incidents reported by other drivers
  • Routes you around traffic, road closures, and accidents
  • Supports toll road avoidance
  • Gets better the more people use it — including you

The map is community-maintained. Regular people — Wazers like you — keep it accurate, up to date, and useful. Every time you drive with Waze running, you're contributing data. When you edit the map, you're directly improving navigation for everyone in South Africa.

Before You Start Editing

Important: Read this before touching the map editor. A few hours of reading now will prevent you from accidentally breaking things that affect thousands of daily commuters.

The golden rule of Waze editing: Usability, Simplicity, Retention.

  • Make it useful for routing
  • Keep it simple and uncluttered
  • Never delete roads — deleting removes traffic history that took years to build

Step 1: Join the Community

Join us on Slack

The South African Waze community communicates primarily via Slack:

  • Server: wazeinternational.slack.com
  • Invite link: bit.ly/WazeIntSlack
  • Join the #south-africa channel

Slack is where you'll get help, request road unlocks, report issues, and connect with Area Managers and Country Managers. It's quicker and more direct than the forums for day-to-day questions.

SA Forum Sections

The SA-specific Discuss forums are organised by province:

  • South Africa (main) — waze.com/discuss/c/4723
  • Gauteng, Western Cape, KwaZulu-Natal, Eastern Cape, Free State
  • Northern Cape, Limpopo, Mpumalanga, North-West
  • National Roads (N-Route Numbers)
  • Standards and Guidelines
  • SA Unlock / Road Closure Requests

Tip: Before posting a new topic, search first. Your question has likely already been answered.

Step 2: Understand Editable Areas

When you log into WME, you can only edit within your editable area:

  • Roads you have driven recently with Waze running
  • Areas within a certain distance of those roads (radius grows with your editor rank)
  • Any area you've been assigned as an Area Manager

Your drive-based editable area expires 90 days after the date of the drive. Keep driving with Waze to maintain your area. Press Shift+E in WME to toggle the editable area layer.

Step 4: Your First Editing Session

What's safe to do when you're new
  • Fix spelling mistakes on road names
  • Correct road direction (one-way vs two-way)
  • Verify which turns are allowed or blocked at intersections
  • Add missing roads you've recently driven
  • Add or correct basic Place information (name, category, address)
What to avoid until you know more
  • Do not delete roads — ever. Not even obvious duplicates. Ask first.
  • Don't create parking lot roads in detail (just entrances and main lanes).
  • Don't touch major highways and their ramps without experience or guidance.
  • Don't modify roads that are locked above your level.
  • Don't split two-way roads into one-way pairs without fully understanding the implications.

Step 5: Drive with Waze On

The simplest way to improve Waze is just to drive around with it running — even when you don't need navigation. Every trip:

  • Records road speeds at different times of day
  • Confirms road layout and direction
  • Expands your editable area

Tip: Your daily commute is enough. You don't need to make special trips.

The Waze Map Editor (WME)

Interface, controls, keyboard shortcuts, and permalinks

What is the Waze Map Editor?

The Waze Map Editor (WME) is the web-based tool for editing the Waze map. Access it at waze.com/editor. You need a Waze account to edit the real map. Practice mode (log out, select Practice) lets you explore without affecting the live map. Use Chrome or Firefox for best results — mobile browsers don't work well.

The Interface at a Glance

  • Top toolbar: Layers icon (top right), Draw road/Roundabout tool, Save button
  • Left panel — nothing selected: Shows tabs for Drives, URs (update requests), and Map Problems
  • Left panel — segment selected: Shows road properties (name, city, type, direction, speed limit, elevation, lock level)
  • Left panel — junction selected: Shows turn restriction options
  • Map area: Click a road to select it. Ctrl+click for multi-select. Drag to pan, scroll to zoom.
🇿🇦 Tip: In South Africa we use Metric, so be sure to go into Settings and change the Units to Metric.

Permalinks

A permalink is a URL that links directly to a specific location in WME or the Livemap. Always share a permalink when asking for help, requesting a lock change, or reporting a map issue.

How to create a permalink

  1. Navigate to the location in WME
  2. Select the segment, junction, or place (optional, but includes it in the link)
  3. Click the Permalink (chain link) icon in the top right of WME
  4. Copy the URL from your browser address bar and share it

Saving Your Work

  • Save regularly — don't batch up hundreds of changes before saving
  • If you get a save error, hover over the save button to identify which change caused it
  • Waze caps point credit at 150 changes per save — save more frequently to get full credit

Tip: Save after every 10–20 significant changes, and always before closing the tab.

Browser Scripts (Tampermonkey)

Some editors use browser scripts via Tampermonkey for extra functionality. These are not official Waze tools but are widely used in the SA community.

  • WME Junction Angle Info: shows what voice instruction drivers hear at a junction. Essential for checking your edits produce correct navigation prompts.
  • WME Toolbox: highlights errors, assists with common editing tasks
  • WME Color Highlights: makes road segment properties easier to see at a glance

Install Tampermonkey for Chrome or Firefox first, then add the script URL. The Junction Angle Info script URL: update.greasyfork.org/scripts/35547/

Road Types, Naming & Speed Limits

How to classify and name roads correctly for South Africa

The SA Numbered Route System

Route FormatExampleDescription
N routes (N1–N18)N1, N3, N14National routes — connect major cities, span provinces. Pentagon signs. Some sections are freeways.
R routes — major (Rxx)R21, R55Major regional routes — two-digit numbers. Diamond-shaped signs. Provincial level.
R routes — minor (Rxxx)R512, R101Minor regional routes — three-digit numbers. Rectangular signs. Regional/local level. Can be paved or gravel.
M routesM1, M5Metropolitan routes — only unique within their city. M1 in Joburg differs from M1 in Cape Town.

Waze Road Types for South Africa

Freeway

A fully controlled-access highway with on/off-ramps, no at-grade intersections, no traffic signals. High-speed, free-flowing traffic.

  • Only numbered routes (N, R, M) can be Freeway type
  • Must be split into two separate one-way segments — one per direction
  • Name format: route number + cardinal direction, e.g. N1 N or N3 S
  • Named freeways: e.g. De Villiers Graaff Motorway (M1 N)
  • Mark only the specific segment at the toll gate/gantry as 'toll road'
  • Do not use Freeway type for inter-town sections that aren't actually access-controlled

Major Highway

National (N) and major regional routes (Rxx) that are not freeways. Connect towns, may pass through town centres, usually multi-lane, few or no signals.

  • All N routes and two-digit R routes should be at least Major Highway type
  • At-grade connections (slip ways) should match the road type they connect to — not Ramp type
  • Name: route number mandatory, e.g. Main Reef Rd (R41 W)

Minor Highway

Three-digit Rxxx routes. Usually single lane, regulated by signals/stop signs. Connects smaller towns.

  • All three-digit R routes should be at least Minor Highway type
  • Name: route number mandatory, e.g. Kliprivier Dr (R556 S)

Primary Street

Non-highway roads carrying heavy traffic — the main arteries through a suburb or town. Examples: Rivonia Road, Jan Smuts Avenue, William Nicol Drive. Metropolitan M routes should typically be at least Primary Street.

Street

Any paved road not fitting into the above categories. Standard suburban and residential roads. Unpaved but well-defined roads should be Street type with the Unpaved checkbox ticked.

Parking Lot Road

Internal roads at parking areas, shopping centres, and office parks. Map the main entrance/exit and lanes leading to shop fronts only. Do not map every individual parking row.

Important: Over-detailed parking lots clutter the map and can generate false traffic jam reports on nearby main roads.

Private Road

Roads not accessible to the general public — gated communities, military bases, private estates. Mark these as Private Road so Waze doesn't route through them. Examples: Dainfern in Fourways, security complexes with multiple entrances.

Ramp

Slip roads connecting the road network to freeways (between terminal ends). Must be one-way, connected at an obtuse angle. Not used as at-grade connectors for major/minor highways.

Road Naming Rules

  • Basic naming: Use the English name and abbreviated suffix — even if the road also has an Afrikaans name
  • Only abbreviate the suffix, not the road name itself — 'Circle Rd' not 'Cir Rd'
  • Proper capitalisation: Rd, St, Ave — not rd, st, ave
  • Be consistent: if a road is '1st Road' on signs, don't name it 'First Road' in Waze
  • Use St. for Saint (e.g. St. Andrews Rd); Dr. for Doctor (e.g. Dr. Mahlangu Ave) — the period after the abbreviation is important

Tip: When in doubt, spell it out. If a suffix abbreviation fails Text-to-Speech, use the full word.

The City Field

Waze has one City field, but SA addresses have multiple levels. Use the Suburb, City format:

  • Correct: Morningside, Sandton
  • Incorrect: Morningside, Johannesburg (old municipality name)
  • Incorrect: Morningside, Sandton, Gauteng, South Africa (too much — country is its own field)

Important: City and suburb names should start with a capital letter. Do not use ALL CAPS or non-standard capitalisation.

Cardinal Directions

Use cardinal directions (N, E, S, W) when a road has two separate carriageways with a median. The standard format is: ROAD NAME (ROUTE NUMBER CARDINAL). Cardinals are spoken aloud by TTS, so drivers hear 'turn onto N1 North' and know which carriageway to use.

Ramp Naming

  • Exit ramps: EXIT NUMBER: ROAD NAME CARDINAL — e.g. Exit 402: N12 E
  • Entry ramps: ROAD NAME > DESTINATION — e.g. N12 > eMalahleni (TTS reads '>' as 'towards')
  • Connecting ramps between entry/exit ramps: leave unnamed (no city, no name)

Tip: Use streetview in WME to verify signage before naming ramps — the signs tell you exactly what to write.

Important: Always verify non-standard limits (e.g. 70 km/h) using streetview before entering them.

Roundabouts (Traffic Circles)

South Africa has many roundabouts. A properly created roundabout gives drivers correct voice prompts.

  • Always use the Roundabout tool in WME (shortcut O) — not just road segments shaped in a circle
  • Set the city name if applicable, but leave the Name field as 'No Name'
  • The road type should match the highest road type connecting to the roundabout
  • For very small 'mini' traffic circles where drivers can go straight over, it's often better not to create a roundabout — the geometry causes map errors

Splitting Dual Carriageways

Important: Think carefully before splitting a two-way road into two one-way segments.

Incorrect splits cause U-turn suggestions, bad routing, and heavy ongoing maintenance at every intersection.

When in doubt, post in the SA Slack channel first.

Creating and Editing Roads

Step-by-step guide to adding and editing roads in WME

Before You Draw Anything

Every road segment in Waze needs all of the following before it works correctly:

  • Correct geometry (shape and position of the road)
  • A road type (Freeway, Primary Street, Street, etc.)
  • A name and city (unless it's a ramp or parking lot road)
  • Correct direction (one-way or two-way)
  • Correct turn permissions at every junction it connects to
  • Saved to the Waze server

Important: If you skip any of these, the road either won't appear in the app or won't route correctly.

Drawing a New Road

Step 1: Start drawing
Hover over the road icon in the top toolbar and click Road, or press I. Your cursor shows a small blue circle — you're in drawing mode. Starting on an existing segment automatically creates a junction there.

Step 2: Follow the road
Click along the path of the road. Each click creates a geometry node. Use satellite imagery or GPS tracks to follow the actual road. Click frequently on curves, just twice for straight sections.

Step 3: Finish drawing
Double-click at the endpoint to finish. The road appears in red — new/unsaved. Press Esc at any time to cancel drawing.

Step 4: Connect the road
Both ends must connect to existing roads (or each other for a loop). A round junction node = connected. A square geometry node = NOT connected.

Tip: If dragging one road onto another won't create a junction, drag it slightly past so they cross, then Ctrl+click both segments to get the intersection popup, and click the crossing point to connect them. Delete the small stub that sticks out.

Setting Road Properties

Address (Name and City)
Click the Edit button (or press E) to edit the address. Fields:

  • Country: Always South Africa
  • City: Use Suburb, City format (e.g. Morningside, Sandton) — or 'None' for roads outside any municipality
  • Street name: Road name with abbreviated suffix (e.g. Main Rd, Church St) — or 'None' for ramps and parking lot roads

Important: Always click Apply after entering address details. If you click elsewhere without applying, your name changes are silently discarded.

Roads with no name and city show as red roads in WME — this is a visual flag that the road needs attention.

Direction

SettingWhen to use
Two-wayNormal streets, most roads
One-way (A→B)One-way streets, one carriageway of a divided road
One-way (B→A)One-way streets going the other direction
UnknownLeave this for the routing engine — only for undriven base map roads

Important: Avoid 'Unknown' for roads you can verify. Unknown roads are auto-adjusted based on how drivers travel them, which can cause brief routing errors.

Speed Limit

Enter the speed limit in km/h. Check that your WME settings use km/h, not mph. Leave ramp speed limits blank unless a sign is visible in streetview.

Elevation

ElevationWhen to use
Ground (0)Default for all normal roads
+1, +2, etc.Road is on a bridge or elevated over another road

Tip: Two roads at the same elevation that overlap will trigger a 'possible junction needed' error. Set them to different levels to avoid false alerts.

Adjusting Road Geometry

To fix the shape of an existing road:

  • Click on the segment to select it
  • Hover over the segment — geometry nodes (white circles) and inter-geometry nodes (light blue dots) appear
  • Drag any node to reposition it
  • Press D while hovering over a node to delete it

Tip: Hold D while moving the mouse over nodes — they delete automatically as the cursor passes. Great for cleaning up straight roads with excess nodes.

Junctions

Moving a junction: Click the junction node (it turns into a blue circle with a 4-way arrow cursor), click and drag to the new location, then adjust any geometry nodes that got pulled out of position.

Removing a two-segment junction (bridging): If a junction connects only two segments and there's no reason for it to exist: click the junction node. If the trash can icon appears at the top right, click it (or press Delete). If no trash can appears, the two segments have different properties — check road type, name, city, direction, or speed limit.

Cutting a segment at a specific point: Draw a short new road starting on the existing segment at the split point, delete the new road you just drew. The junction stays — the original segment is now split at that point.

Turn Restrictions

Turn restrictions control which directions drivers are allowed to go at a junction. Green arrows = allowed. Red arrows = not allowed.

Setting turns: Click a segment to see its turn arrows. Click a red arrow to allow that turn (turns green). Click a green arrow to disallow it (turns red).

Allowing all turns at a junction: Click the junction node (not a segment). Click Allow all turns in the left panel, or press W.

// Best practice for new junctions

Important: Always do this after creating a new junction:

1. Click the junction node

2. Press Q to disallow all turns

3. Press W to allow all turns

4. Then manually restrict any turns not allowed in real life.

// The Q+W sequence clears invisible 'soft' restrictions that silently block valid routing.

Tip: Press Shift+Z to toggle a view showing all disallowed turns across every visible junction. Zoom out to ~500m, scan the area, and fix any red arrows that shouldn't be there.

Saving

Click the Save button or press Ctrl+S. Save every 10–20 meaningful changes. If you get a save error, hover over the save button to identify the cause. If you can't resolve the error, undo recent changes one by one until the save works.

Locking, Places, Toll Roads & Roles

SA lock levels, points of interest, toll roads, and community roles

Segment Locking

Locking a segment protects it from being accidentally edited by lower-ranked editors. Only lock a road once it has been verified as correct — correct geometry, name, road type, and turn restrictions.

SA Lock Level Guidelines

Road typeMinimum lock level
Freeway (N routes) including ramps and junctionsLevel 5
Major Highway (two-digit R or N routes)Level 4
Minor Highway (three-digit R routes)Level 3
Primary StreetsLevel 2
Standard StreetsLevel 1 (default)
Toll gantry/gantry segmentsLevel 5 or higher

Special Situations

  • Roads not matching aerials: Lock to L4 if geometry is confirmed correct but imagery is outdated
  • Unexpected configurations: Lock unusual but intentional features to prevent reversion; add a map comment explaining why
  • Vandalism: Lock after discussion with the community and ZA Country Admin approval

Note: Level 6 locks are not currently used in South Africa as there are insufficient Level 6 editors in the country.

Places (Points of Interest)

Area vs. Point:
Area place: Physical footprint larger than 40m x 40m (smallest size visible on app at max zoom)
Point place: Smaller than 40m x 40m, or businesses inside a larger building or complex

Minimum info required for L1 lock: Category, Name, Street, City, Country

Extended info for a fully completed place (L3): All basic information plus: linked Google Place, website, phone number, opening times, relevant facilities

Toll Roads in South Africa

The Waze 'Avoid Toll Roads' option is active in South Africa. Any segment where a driver must pay must be correctly marked for Waze to offer alternative routing.

How to mark a toll road

  • Check the Toll road checkbox in the segment properties for the specific segment at the toll gate/gantry
  • Do NOT mark the entire highway as toll — only the segment at the actual gantry location
  • Add a junction node at the toll location to prevent false traffic jam pop-ups

Best Practices & Edits to Avoid

What to do, what not to do, and SA-specific tips from the community

The Core Principle Keep it simple. Make it useful. Don't lose data. Every edit you make affects real commuters. A well-intentioned but incorrect edit can send hundreds of drivers the wrong way. An accidental deletion can wipe out years of traffic data. When you're unsure, ask in Slack before editing.

General Best Practices

1. Verify before you edit: Don't just fix what looks wrong — confirm it's actually wrong using Streetview to check signage, GPS tracks layer (Shift+G), Satellite imagery (Shift+I), or Livemap to test actual routing.

2. Fix turn restrictions every time you create a junction: Click the junction node, press Q (disallow all), press W (allow all), and manually restrict any turns not allowed in real life. Skipping this leaves invisible 'soft' turn restrictions.

3. Use Shift+Z after working on an area: This shows all disallowed turns on every visible junction. Zoom out to ~100–500m, scan the area, and fix any red arrows that shouldn't be there.

4. Keep geometry clean: Delete unnecessary nodes on straight roads (hover + press D). Excess nodes slow the database. After adjusting geometry, verify your changes against satellite imagery.

5. Save early, save often: Save after every meaningful group of changes to limit work lost and ensure edits go live sooner.

6. Don't add new segments into busy roads: When adding a connecting road or fixing a junction in the middle of an existing road, split the existing segment rather than drawing a new one from scratch. Splitting preserves data history.

7. Test routing on Livemap: For anything involving major roads, junctions, or turn restrictions — open Livemap and test the route you expect people to take.

Edits to Avoid

Deleting roads: Never delete a road unless you are 100% certain it doesn't exist. Deleting a road deletes all traffic data — this cannot be recovered. If a road has been realigned, move/edit the existing segment.

Tracing from Google Maps: This is a copyright violation. Use Waze's satellite imagery and GPS tracks instead.

Excessive parking lot detail: Map the entrance, exit, and main internal lanes only. Don't draw every individual parking row. Over-detailed parking lots clutter the map.

Splitting roads unnecessarily: Do not split a two-way road into one-way pairs unless the physical road is a genuine divided carriageway. Unnecessary splits require heavy maintenance.

Setting direction to 'Unknown': Unknown direction should only exist on undriven base map roads. Always set a definite direction for any road you're actively editing.

Ignoring update requests: User-submitted update requests (URs) are real driver problems. Check them, investigate, and close them with an explanation — never close a UR without checking what it's about.

SA-Specific Tips from the Community

1. Gated communities and private roads: South Africa has many gated communities, security estates, and complexes. These must be marked as Private Road type. Missing private road tags are a common source of URs.

2. Farm roads and 4x4 trails: Private farm roads and dedicated 4x4 trails serve no purpose for general commuters. Don't add them to Waze. If already mapped, use Street type with the Unpaved checkbox.

3. Roads renamed to honour new leaders: South Africa regularly renames roads. When renaming happens: change the primary name to the new name, add the old name as an alternate name until old signage is fully replaced. Primary name should always match current signage.

4. Municipal boundary changes: Don't use old municipality names. Many areas were consolidated into metros. Use the current municipal name — for example, Bryanston's city field should be 'Bryanston, Sandton', not 'Bryanston, Johannesburg'.

5. Connecting roads at junctions — the safe approach: Draw the junction, press Q (disallow all), press W (allow all), and manually restrict what's genuinely not allowed. This 10-second process prevents days of routing problems.

6. Scripts save time: WME Junction Angle Info shows what instruction a driver will hear at any junction. Use it constantly when creating new connections.

Checking for Problems

Map Problems (automatic): Waze automatically flags: possible missing junctions, possible turn restrictions, and long unnamed segments. Check the Map Problems layer (Shift+P) regularly in your area.

Update Requests (user-submitted): Users submit URs when the map is wrong or routing failed. Click the UR to see the description and location, test routing in Livemap, use conversations to communicate if needed, and close with an explanation once resolved.
