Welcome & Getting Started

Your first steps into the SA Waze editing community

What is Waze?

Waze is a free GPS navigation app for your smartphone. Beyond just giving you directions, it provides spoken turn-by-turn directions with street names, handles real-time traffic incidents, routes you around closures, and supports toll avoidance. It gets better the more people use it — including you.

The map is entirely community-maintained. Regular people keep it accurate and up to date. Every time you drive with Waze running, you contribute data, but when you edit the map, you directly improve navigation for everyone in South Africa.

The Golden Rule of Waze Editing

Usability, Simplicity, Retention. Make it useful for routing, keep it simple and uncluttered, and never delete roads — deleting removes traffic history that took years to build.

Step 1: Join the Community

The South African Waze community communicates primarily via Slack on wazeinternational.slack.com. Use the invite link bit.ly/WazeIntSlack and join the #south-africa channel. This is where you get quick help, request unlocks, and connect with Area and Country Managers.

Step 2: Understand Editable Areas

You can only edit within your editable area, which includes roads you have driven recently with Waze running (and a radius around them that grows with your rank) or areas assigned to you as an Area Manager. This drive-based area expires 90 days after the drive date. Press Shift+E in WME to toggle this layer view.

The Waze Map Editor (WME)

Interface, controls, keyboard shortcuts, and permalinks

Access the real editor at waze.com/editor using Chrome or Firefox. Log out and choose "Practice mode" if you want to test changes safely without touching the live network.

Tip for SA Editors: We use Metric units! Go into your WME Settings immediately and change the Units to Metric.

How to Share a Permalink

A permalink is a URL linking to a specific spot in WME. Always share one when asking for help or requesting a lock change. To create one: navigate to your location, select the target segment/junction, click the Permalink (chain link) icon in the top right, and copy the URL from your browser's address bar.

Road Types, Naming & Speed Limits

How to classify and name roads correctly for South Africa

Waze Road Classification Standards

Freeway (120 km/h) Controlled-access highways with ramps and no traffic signals. Only numbered routes (N, R, M) can be Freeways. They must be split into two separate one-way segments. Format: N1 N or De Villiers Graaff Motorway (M1 N).
Major Highway (100–120 km/h) National (N) and major regional routes (Rxx) that aren't access-controlled. Name format requires route numbers: Main Reef Rd (R41 W).
Minor Highway (80–100 km/h) Three-digit Rxxx routes. Usually single lane with stops/signals connecting smaller towns: Kliprivier Dr (R556 S).
Primary Street (60–80 km/h) & Street (40 km/h) Primary Streets are main suburban arteries like Jan Smuts Avenue. Streets are standard paved suburban roads. For unpaved but well-defined roads, use Street type and check the Unpaved box.
Private Road & Parking Lot Road Private Roads are for gated/security communities (e.g., Dainfern) so Waze won't route general commuters through them. For Parking Lots, map only the main entrance/exit and primary lanes leading to storefronts — never map individual parking rows. Speed limits are left blank on these types.

Road Naming Suffixes (SA Standards)

Use the English name and abbreviated suffix with proper capitalization, even if the road has an Afrikaans name. Include a period for title abbreviations (e.g., St. for Saint, Dr. for Doctor).

Avenue → Ave
Boulevard → Blvd
Drive → Dr
Road → Rd
Street → St
Crescent → Cres
Circle → Cir
Close → Spell Out (TTS fails Cl)

The City Field Format

Waze uses a single city field. In South Africa, use the Suburb, City format. Do not use old municipality names or include provinces/countries.

  • Correct: Morningside, Sandton
  • Incorrect: Morningside, Johannesburg (Old municipality name)
  • Incorrect: Morningside, Sandton, Gauteng, South Africa (Too much information)

Creating & Editing Roads

Step-by-step guide to drawing and configuring segments

Drawing a New Road

  1. Press I to enter drawing mode. Your cursor will turn into a small blue circle.
  2. Click along the path of the road to create geometry nodes. Click frequently on curves, but only twice for straight stretches.
  3. Double-click at the final endpoint to finish. Unsaved roads will appear in a bright red hue.
  4. Ensure both ends connect properly to the network. A round junction node means it's fully connected; a square node means it is disconnected.
Crucial Step: Always click Apply after modifying address or name details in the left panel. If you click elsewhere on the map without clicking apply first, your alterations will be silently discarded without warning.

Setting the Elevation Level

To prevent false map conflict errors, overlapping segments must have distinct elevation values assigned if they don't intersect physically:

Ground (0) Standard roads
+1, +2 Bridges / Overpasses
-1, -2 Tunnels / Underpasses
-5 Non-drivable trails

The Ultimate New Junction Sequence (Q+W)

To eliminate hidden, invisible "soft restrictions" that can break navigation routing entirely, execute this sequence every single time you establish a new intersection:

# Click the core junction node itself, then:

Press Q → Disallows all turns entirely (all turn arrows flash red)

Press W → Allows all turns cleanly (all turn arrows switch to green)

# Now manually click and restrict (turn red) the specific paths banned in real life.

Locking, Places & Toll Roads

SA lock standards, points of interest, and toll management

South Africa Segment Lock Guidelines

Locking protects verified configurations from accidental edits by lower-ranked users. Do not lock a road until geometry, names, direction, and turn settings are 100% verified.

Road Classification Type Minimum Lock Level Required
Freeways (N-Routes) & associated rampsLevel 5
Toll gate / Toll plaza mainline segmentsLevel 5 or higher
Major Highways (Two-digit R or N routes)Level 4
Minor Highways (Three-digit R routes)Level 3
Primary Streets (Main community arteries)Level 2
Standard Streets (Suburban residential roads)Level 1 (Default)
Note: Level 6 locks are not utilized dynamically in SA because there are insufficient Level 6 editors active in the country.

Mapping Toll Roads Accurately

South Africa features extensive toll networks (SANRAL, Bakwena, N3TC, TRAC, Entilini). To map them without routing issues:

  • Check the Toll road checkbox ONLY on the specific short segment directly containing the physical gate or gantry.
  • Never mark the entire length of the highway as a toll road — doing so breaks alternative routing calculations.
  • Add a junction node at the toll location to stop false traffic congestion pop-ups from appearing for stationary vehicles paying fees.

Best Practices & Edits to Avoid

SA-specific tips compiled from veteran local community contributors

Never Trace from Google Maps!

Tracing geometry directly from Google Maps or using their satellite tiles constitutes copyright infringement. Use Waze's officially licensed imagery or real user GPS tracks (Shift+G) instead.

Handling Renamed South African Roads Local routes are regularly renamed to honor historical leaders. When a name change occurs, update the primary name field to match the fresh street signage, and relegate the previous name to the alternate name field until regional signage is entirely uniform.
Farm Roads & 4x4 Trails Private farm routes or dedicated recreational 4x4 tracks do not serve everyday commuters. Do not add them to the map. If they are already present, assign them as a standard Street type with the Unpaved box checked. Never use the legacy 'Off-road' designation.
Splitting Existing Segments Safely When linking a brand new road to an established, busy main road, split the existing segment rather than creating a whole new block from scratch. Splitting preserves historic speed data, whereas fresh segments wipe traffic history clean, causing routing recalculation errors.
